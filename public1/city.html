History of Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad, founded in 1411 by Sultan Ahmed Shah, is a city rich in history and culture. Known as the 'Manchester of India' for its textile industry, Ahmedabad is also celebrated for its well-preserved architecture, ranging from ancient mosques and temples to historic stepwells and gates. The city was recognized as India's first UNESCO World Heritage City in 2017, owing to its rich architectural legacy and vibrant cultural heritage. From the tranquil Sabarmati Ashram to the bustling markets of the old city, Ahmedabad offers a unique glimpse into India's past and present.

Adalaj Stepwell

A beautifully crafted stepwell built in 1498 with an elaborate design, serving as a resting place for many travelers.

Sabarmati Ashram

Once the residence of Mahatma Gandhi, this ashram on the banks of the Sabarmati River is now a national monument.

Jama Masjid

A beautiful mosque built in 1424 during the reign of Ahmed Shah I, known for its stunning architecture.

Sidi Saiyyed Mosque

Famous for its intricately carved stone latticework windows, especially the tree of life motif.

Kite Museum

Located in Sanskar Kendra, it houses unique and rare collections of kites from around the world.

Swaminarayan Temple

A stunning Hindu temple complex with intricate carvings and vibrant marble structures dedicated to Lord Swaminarayan.
